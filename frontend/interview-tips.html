Research the Company Thoroughly

Know the company's products, services, recent news, culture, and values. Check their LinkedIn, website, and recent press releases. Understand their competitors and market position.

Master Your Resume

Be ready to explain every line on your resume. Prepare stories for each project with the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result). Quantify your achievements.

Think Out Loud

For technical problems, verbalize your thought process. Interviewers want to see HOW you think, not just the final answer. Discuss trade-offs and alternatives.

Handle 'I Don't Know' Gracefully

If you don't know something, be honest but show problem-solving skills. Say what you DO know, how you'd find the answer, or draw parallels to similar concepts.

Practice Coding on Paper/Whiteboard

If there's a coding round, practice writing code without IDE assistance. Focus on clean, readable code with proper variable names and comments.

Know Your Fundamentals

Review core CS concepts: Data Structures, Algorithms, OOP, DBMS, OS, and Networks. Know the time/space complexity of common operations.

Show Leadership & Initiative

Even without formal leadership roles, highlight times you took initiative, helped teammates, or drove a project forward. Showcase proactive behavior.

Demonstrate Problem-Solving

Share examples where you faced challenges and overcame them. Focus on your analytical approach, creativity, and persistence.

Show Cultural Fit

Research company values and weave them into your answers. Show alignment with their mission while being authentic to yourself.

Handle Weaknesses Smartly

Pick a real weakness but show how you're actively working to improve it. Avoid clich√©s like 'I'm a perfectionist' or strengths disguised as weaknesses.

Speaking Negatively

Never badmouth previous employers, professors, or teammates. It reflects poorly on your professionalism and attitude.

Being Too Humble or Arrogant

Strike a balance. Don't downplay your achievements, but also don't take all credit for team efforts. Use 'we' for team wins, 'I' for individual contributions.

Not Asking Questions

Saying 'I don't have any questions' signals lack of interest. Always have questions prepared, even if many were answered during the interview.

Arriving Unprepared

Not knowing basic company information or being unable to discuss your own resume are red flags. Preparation is non-negotiable.

Rambling or Being Too Brief

Avoid lengthy, unfocused answers OR one-word responses. Aim for 1-2 minute structured answers for behavioral questions.
